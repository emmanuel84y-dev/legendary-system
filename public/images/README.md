
# Pinnacle Consulting Asset Guide

To ensure your website displays images correctly, please follow these instructions for naming and placing your image files.

## Directory Structure
Place all images in a folder named `images` at the root of your project.

## Naming Conventions & Aspect Ratios

### 1. General Branding & Layout
- `mountains-icon.svg`: The primary logo icon (used in the Navbar).
- `background.jpg`: The high-resolution hero background image.
  - **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 or wider (Landscape)
  - **Min Resolution**: 1920x1080px
- `about-team.jpg`: The main image for the "Our Foundation" / About section.
  - **Aspect Ratio**: 4:3 or 16:9 (Landscape)
  - **Min Resolution**: 1200x800px

### 2. Service Detail Pages (New!)
These images appear as the full-width header for each service page.
- `service-strategy.jpg`: Header for Strategy Consulting.
- `service-ops.jpg`: Header for Operations Optimization.
- `service-hr.jpg`: Header for HR & Talent Advisory.
- `service-growth.jpg`: Header for Growth Solutions.
  - **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 or 21:9 (Ultrawide)
  - **Min Resolution**: 1920x800px

### 3. Insights (Blog Posts)
- `strategy1.jpg`: "5 Strategies for Sustainable Business Growth" article image.
- `strategy2.jpg`: "The Future of HR" article image.
- `strategy3.jpg`: "Optimize Operations" article image.
  - **Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 or 4:3
  - **Min Resolution**: 800x600px

### 4. Team (Our Staff)
- `team-marcus.jpg`: Marcus Thorne (Managing Director).
- `team-elena.jpg`: Elena Vance (Chief Strategy Officer).
- `team-david.jpg`: David Chen (Director of Operations).
- `team-sarah.jpg`: Sarah Jenkins (HR Lead).
- `team-amara.jpg`: Amara Okafor (Innovation Partner).
  - **Aspect Ratio**: 1:1 (Square) or 3:4 (Portrait)
  - **Min Resolution**: 400x500px

### 5. Case Studies
- `case-1.jpg` through `case-12.jpg`: (Optional) Unique images for each case study card.

## Pro Tips
- **Resolution**: Use images at least 1920x1080px for backgrounds and headers.
- **Format**: `.jpg` is best for photographs, `.png` or `.svg` for icons/logos.
- **Compression**: Use a tool like TinyJPG to reduce file size without losing quality for faster loading.
